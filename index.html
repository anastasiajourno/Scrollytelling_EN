This is one of the oldest schools in Kyrgyzstan.
Cracks are crawling on its walls.
Studying in such a school is life-threatening.
Here it is.
But this is by far not the only such school.
There are 204 dilapidated schools.
This is every tenth school in the country.
172 of them can no longer be repaired.
Every day children and teachers in these schools risk their life. But that’s not all.
Another 395 schools in the country are in need of major repairs.
In many schools, there have been no major repairs since the day the buildings were put into operation.
More than a quarter of schools in Kyrgyzstan are in a deplorable condition.
At least three hundred thousand children study in these schools every day. And that, too, is not all.
More than 80% of schools in Kyrgyzstan are “structurally unsafe”, according to a nationwide assessment of the safety of schools and preschool educational institutions in Kyrgyzstan, done by the Ministry of Education and Science and UNICEF back in 2011.
10 years later, we did our research and discovered nothing has changed.
All over the country children study, and teachers work, risking their lives.
Why hasn’t the issue been addressed for years?
